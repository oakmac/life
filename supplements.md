# Supplement Stack

I am a 40-something healthy male. I am often asked about the supplements I take, so here it is documented. My wife also takes a similar stack.

## Glossary

**Cost**
- `$` — inexpensive, easy to find
- `$$` — moderate cost
- `$$$` — expensive

**Priority**
- `essential` — foundational nutrition. Strong evidence, most people are deficient or cannot reliably get enough from diet alone. You should probably be taking this regardless of your goals.
- `recommended` — good evidence for meaningful benefit, worth the cost and effort, but more targeted. You're optimizing, not just covering your bases.
- `why not` — low cost, low risk, some upside. The evidence may be thinner or the benefit modest, but there's no good reason to skip it.

## Preface

The supplement industry is a mess. They sell a lot of stuff that you don't need, at large margins, and often combining ingredients in weird ways. People are misinformed and are being marketed to.

**Good example:** selling 1,000mg tablets of Vitamin C is silly.

Vitamin C absorption is dose-dependent:

| Dose | Absorption |
|------|------------|
| ≤200mg | ~90% |
| 500mg | ~70–75% |
| 1,000mg | ~50% or less |
| Beyond that | expensive urine |

So why do companies sell 1,000mg tablets? Marketing. "More is better!"

**All of this is optional.** You get what you need from the food you eat. It's literally in the name: *supplemental*. Don't major in minor things. Macronutrient quantity and composition will have a far greater impact on what you look like and how you feel than any supplement(s) you take.

That said — I do feel better when I take this stack. And I take it every day. I especially pay attention to it when I am cutting (eating reduced macronutrients for the purpose of losing fat) to ensure proper nutrition.

## Fat-Soluble vs Water-Soluble

Certain vitamins are water-soluble and others are fat-soluble.

- **Water-soluble** — take anytime, just with water.
- **Fat-soluble** — take with dietary fat, both for absorption and to prevent upset stomach. It doesn't need to be much — a handful of almonds is enough. If you've ever taken a supplement and felt stomach unease, you likely consumed a fat-soluble vitamin without food.

## The Stack

---

### Morning — with water / coffee / empty stomach

- **Vitamin C** — 500mg · `$` · `essential`
  - Water-soluble, inexpensive
  - Really no reason *not* to take this
  - Form does not matter (ascorbic acid is easy to synthesize)
  - No reason to take more than 500mg in a single sitting — already on the higher end of what gets absorbed

- **Thorne B Complex** · `$$` · `essential`
  - Water-soluble
  - More expensive, but in bioavailable form — key ingredients like B12 (Methylcobalamin), B6 (Pyridoxal 5'-Phosphate), B2 (Riboflavin 5'-Phosphate), and Folate (L-5-MTHF) are the active forms your body can use directly, unlike the cheaper synthetic forms in most B vitamins
  - Makes your pee yellow due to riboflavin
  - Per capsule: Biotin 400mcg · Choline (Citrate) 28mg · Folate (L-5-MTHF) 667mcg DFE · B1 (Thiamin HCl) 110mg · B12 (Methylcobalamin) 400mcg · B2 (Riboflavin 5'-Phosphate) 10mg · B3 (Niacin) 10mg · B3 (Niacinamide) 130mg · B5 (Pantothenic Acid) 110mg · B6 (Pyridoxal 5'-Phosphate) 10mg

- **Taurine** — 1,000mg · `$` · `recommended`
  - Amino acid that is conditionally essential — your body makes some, but not always enough
  - Supports mitochondrial function and cardiovascular health (blood pressure, arterial flexibility)
  - Reduces muscle cramping and improves endurance
  - Antioxidant properties; reduces oxidative stress
  - Common ingredient in energy drinks for the non-stimulant energy and focus effect

- **Alpha-GPC** (L-alpha-glycerylphosphorylcholine) — 600mg · `$$` · `recommended`
  - 300mg in the morning, 300mg early afternoon — staggered for more consistent acetylcholine support throughout the day
  - Most bioavailable form of choline, an essential nutrient most people under-eat (eggs are the main dietary source)
  - Precursor to acetylcholine — your primary learning and memory neurotransmitter
  - Taken as a nootropic for focus and mental clarity
  - Acetylcholine is also key for muscle contraction; some evidence for power output
  - Critical for fat metabolism in the liver

---

### Mid-Morning

- **Creatine** — 10–15g · `$` · `recommended`
  - Powder mixed with yogurt, or pills
  - Recent research indicates brain health benefits at doses above 5g per day
  - Rated `recommended` because most people think of this as a gym supplement, but the evidence for cognitive and general health benefits is strong enough that it could reasonably be called `essential`.

---

### Afternoon — with dietary fat

- **Vitamin D** — 125mcg (5,000 IU) as D3 Cholecalciferol · `$` · `essential`
  - Most people are deficient, particularly those who spend time indoors or live somewhere without year-round sun
  - Drives calcium absorption — without adequate D, calcium doesn't get into bones properly
  - Critical for immune function; D receptors are on virtually every immune cell
  - Deficiency is strongly correlated with depression and low mood
  - Some evidence that correcting deficiency supports healthy testosterone levels
  - The RDA (600–800 IU) is widely considered too low; 5,000 IU is a common corrective/maintenance dose
  - Take with K2 — D mobilizes calcium, K2 directs it into bones rather than arteries

- **Vitamin K** · `$` · `essential`
  - A well-designed K supplement — most cheap products only include K1 or a single form of K2. Having all three in one capsule is the right approach.
  - K1 as Phytonadione — 120mcg
  - K2 as Menaquinone-4 (MK-4) — 360mcg
  - K2 as Menaquinone-7 (MK-7) as MenaQ7® — 90mcg
  - MenaQ7® is a patented, clinically studied form of MK-7 — the form used in most of the research on K2 and cardiovascular and bone health

> **Note:** Always take Vitamin D and K together — they work synergistically in the body.

- **Kirkland Multivitamin** · `$` · `why not`
  - Inexpensive; covers bases for trace nutrients you might miss in your normal diet (selenium, chromium, molybdenum, etc.)

- **CoQ10** — 200mg Kaneka Ubiquinol · `$$$` · `recommended`
  - Two forms exist: ubiquinone and ubiquinol
  - The body converts ubiquinone → ubiquinol, but this conversion becomes less efficient with age
  - If you are under age 40, ubiquinone is probably fine. If 40+, take ubiquinol.

- **Nordic Naturals Ultimate Omega** — 2–3 capsules · `$$` · `essential`
  - EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid) 650mg
  - DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid) 450mg
  - Always store in the fridge — fish oil oxidizes and goes rancid at room temperature
