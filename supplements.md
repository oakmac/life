# Supplement Stack

I am a 40-something healthy male. I am often asked about the supplements I take, so here it is documented. My wife also takes a similar stack.

## Glossary

**Cost**
- `$` вЂ” inexpensive, easy to find
- `$$` вЂ” moderate cost
- `$$$` вЂ” expensive

**Priority**
- `essential` вЂ” foundational nutrition. Strong evidence, most people are deficient or cannot reliably get enough from diet alone. You should probably be taking this regardless of your goals.
- `recommended` вЂ” good evidence for meaningful benefit, worth the cost and effort, but more targeted. You're optimizing, not just covering your bases.
- `why not` вЂ” low cost, low risk, some upside. The evidence may be thinner or the benefit modest, but there's no good reason to skip it.

**Product**
- `product matters` вЂ” formulation, bioavailability, or sourcing meaningfully affects what you're getting. The specific product is part of the recommendation.
- `generic ok` вЂ” the ingredient is the ingredient. Buy the cheapest thing you can find.

## Preface

The supplement industry is a mess. They sell a lot of stuff that you don't need, at large margins, and often combining ingredients in weird ways. People are misinformed and are being marketed to.

**Good example:** selling 1,000mg tablets of Vitamin C is silly.

Vitamin C absorption is dose-dependent:

| Dose | Absorption |
|------|------------|
| в‰¤200mg | ~90% |
| 500mg | ~70вЂ“75% |
| 1,000mg | ~50% or less |
| Beyond that | expensive urine |

So why do companies sell 1,000mg tablets? Marketing. "More is better!"

**All of this is optional.** You get what you need from the food you eat. It's literally in the name: *supplemental*. Don't major in minor things. Macronutrient quantity and composition will have a far greater impact on what you look like and how you feel than any supplement(s) you take.

That said вЂ” I do feel better when I take this stack. And I take it every day. I especially pay attention to it when I am cutting (eating reduced macronutrients for the purpose of losing fat) to ensure proper nutrition.

## Fat-Soluble vs Water-Soluble

Certain vitamins are water-soluble and others are fat-soluble.

- **Water-soluble** вЂ” take anytime, just with water.
- **Fat-soluble** вЂ” take with dietary fat, both for improved absorption and to prevent upset stomach. It doesn't need to be much вЂ” a handful of almonds is enough. If you've ever taken a supplement and felt stomach unease, you likely consumed a fat-soluble vitamin without food.
